## What Is CMI Coursework and Why Do Students Need Help
CMI coursework consists of the assessed units that make up a CMI qualification. Each unit produces a written assignment - a management report, essay, reflective account, or case study - assessed against CMI's Learning Outcomes and Assessment Criteria. The qualification type determines how many units a student must complete.

CMI qualifications are workplace-based. Students are expected to apply management theory to their own professional context - drawing on real examples from their organisation, their team, and their management experience. This makes CMI distinct from a standard academic course: the content must be both theoretically grounded and practically applied, within a word count, formatted to academic standards, with Harvard referencing, and submitted to a deadline - all while working full-time in a management role.

For most CMI students, the challenge is not understanding the management content. It is the academic writing standard required to express that understanding in a way that satisfies the Assessment Criteria.

### CMI Award, Certificate, and Diploma: How Much Coursework Is Involved
| Qualification Type | Units Required | Approx. Total Word Count | Typical Student Profile |
|---|---|---|---|
| CMI Award | 1 unit | 2,000–3,500 words | Manager testing CMI before committing to a full qualification |
| CMI Certificate | 2–3 units | 6,000–10,500 words | Manager building a formal qualification foundation |
| CMI Diploma | 6–13 units | 15,000–40,000+ words | Manager committed to a comprehensive, multi-unit qualification |

The Diploma carries the most significant coursework load. A Level 5 Diploma student typically completes 6–8 units - approximately 18,000–30,000 words - over 12–24 months, alongside a full-time management role. A Level 7 Diploma student faces the same workload at a higher academic standard: 5,000–6,500 words per unit, Critically Analytical writing, and 15–20 peer-reviewed sources per submission.
