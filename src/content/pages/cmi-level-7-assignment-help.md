## What Is CMI Level 7 and Who Studies It?

CMI Level 7 qualifies senior leaders, directors, and C-suite executives who need to develop and demonstrate strategic management capability at the highest formal credential level available through CMI. It is equivalent to a postgraduate or Master's qualification on the UK National Qualifications Framework - the same academic level as an MBA or MSc in Management.

The typical CMI Level 7 student occupies a strategic leadership role: director, VP, CEO, NHS Band 8b+ executive, military senior officer, government department head, chief executive of a public body or social enterprise, or senior partner in a professional services firm. These are leaders who set strategy, manage budgets in the millions, and are accountable to boards and commissioners, not managers who supervise teams.

The critical distinction at Level 7 is scope. Level 5 Management and Leadership operates at the level of a team, department, or operational function. Level 7 Strategic Management and Leadership operates at the level of the organisation, the sector, or the board. Assignments do not ask how a manager handled a team challenge. They ask how an executive designed, led, or evaluated an organisational strategy. This distinction defines everything about what Level 7 assignments must contain.

## What CMI Level 7 Assignments Require

CMI Level 7 assignments require Master's level strategic analysis - the most academically demanding standard in the CMI qualification framework. The primary format is a strategic management paper or strategic leadership report, structurally and substantively different from the management reports used at Level 5.

**Word count per unit** is typically 5,000–6,500 words. Units 712 (Strategic Management Project) and 713 (Applied Research) extend to 8,000–12,000 words.

**Referencing** requires 15–20 academic sources per unit, with a strong emphasis on peer-reviewed management research, empirical case studies, strategic leadership theory texts, and government or sector-level strategic frameworks. ManagementDirect and general management textbooks are insufficient at Level 7: assessors expect engagement with the research literature.

### Critically Analyse at Level 7: What It Requires at Strategic Depth

Critically Analyse is the defining command verb at Level 7 - and the most demanding command verb in the entire CMI framework.

**Analyse** (Level 4): breaks a concept into components and examines their relationships.

**Critically Evaluate** (Level 6): evaluates with criteria, acknowledges limitations, engages with counterarguments.

**Critically Analyse** (Level 7): decomposes the strategic concept or problem into its components, applies empirical evidence to each, identifies the limitations and assumptions of the frameworks used, engages with conflicting research and competing theoretical perspectives, and synthesises an original strategic position - a view that goes beyond summarising what the literature says.

In practice: a Level 5 assignment might Evaluate transformational leadership using Bass and Avolio's model against the criteria of team motivation and performance. A Level 7 assignment Critically Analyses transformational leadership - examining its conceptual foundations, the empirical evidence base across sectors and cultures, critiques from scholars questioning its universal validity (e.g., evidence that transactional leadership outperforms transformational in crisis and compliance-heavy environments), and synthesising a strategic position on where and under what conditions transformational leadership provides competitive advantage at an organisational level.

The Level 7 distinction is not more content. It is a qualitatively different mode of academic engagement.

### Strategic Papers vs Management Reports: Level 7 vs Level 5 Format

| Feature | Level 5 Management Report | Level 7 Strategic Paper |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | Team, department, or operational function | Organisation, sector, or board level |
| Focus | Applying management theory to an operational scenario | Critically analysing strategic theory and developing strategic options |
| Format | Executive summary + analysis + SMART recommendations | Strategic analysis + options evaluation + strategic recommendation with governance implications |
| Command verbs | Evaluate, Justify | Critically Analyse, Justify at strategic depth, Develop and Implement a Strategy |
| References | 10–12 sources - management textbooks, CMI publications | 15–20 sources - peer-reviewed journals, empirical research, strategic frameworks |
| Word count | 3,000–5,000 words | 5,000–6,500 words |

Students who approach Level 7 as an extended Level 5 management report consistently produce work that misses the strategic scope, command verb depth, and research standard the assessor requires. This is the most common source of referrals at Level 7.

CMI Level 5 and Level 7 are the two most-searched CMI levels - and the most frequently compared. The five key differences are:

**1: Scope**
Level 5 addresses team, department, or operational management scenarios. Level 7 addresses organisational, sector, or board-level strategic decisions. A Level 5 assignment might Evaluate a change management approach for a team restructure. A Level 7 assignment Critically Analyses a strategic transformation programme across an entire organisation.

**2: Format**
Level 5 uses a management report format - executive summary, analysis sections, SMART recommendations. Level 7 uses a strategic paper - strategic analysis, critically evaluated strategic options, governance implications, organisational alignment.

**3: Command verbs**
Level 5 requires Evaluate (criteria + evidence + judgement) and Justify (evidence-backed reasoning). Level 7 requires Critically Analyse (all components of a strategic issue, with competing evidence, theoretical limitations, and original strategic synthesis) and Justify at strategic depth (industry evidence, competitive analysis, research literature).

**4: Academic sources**
Level 5: 10–12 sources, management textbooks and CMI publications. Level 7: 15–20 sources, peer-reviewed journals, empirical research, strategic frameworks.

**5: Writer credential**
Level 5 is written by practising managers with CMI Level 5+ qualifications. Level 7 is written by strategic leaders and directors with CMI Level 7 or postgraduate management qualifications and executive leadership experience.

CMI Level 6 sits between the two - requiring Critically Evaluate rather than Critically Analyse. Our [CMI Level 6 assignment help](/cmi-level-6-assignment-help/) is the intermediate option for Professional Management and Leadership.
