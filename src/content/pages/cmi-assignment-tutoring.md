## What Is CMI Assignment Tutoring and Who Is It For
CMI assignment tutoring guides you through the writing process with expert support at every stage: planning, drafting, and revision. You remain the author of your work. The tutor provides the academic direction: how to structure your response to the unit brief, how to apply the correct command verb at the right depth, which management theories apply and how to connect them to your workplace context, and where your draft falls short of the Assessment Criteria.

Tutoring works at every stage of the writing process: before you write, while you're writing, and after you've written a draft that needs improvement.

### Who Benefits Most from CMI Assignment Tutoring
**Students who can write but need academic structure**: You understand your management topic from professional experience. The challenge is translating that knowledge into academic language, referencing it correctly, and applying command verbs like Evaluate and Justify at the depth CMI assessors expect. A tutor bridges that gap without replacing your voice.

**Students on resubmission after a referral**: You have received assessor feedback identifying unmet Learning Outcomes. You need targeted, precise guidance on exactly what to rewrite, not a generic improvement. Resubmission coaching is one of our most requested tutoring formats.

**Students studying for professional development, not just the certificate**: You want to genuinely develop your academic and analytical skills alongside the qualification. Tutoring builds those skills; writing services don't.

**First-time Level 5 Diploma students**: Transitioning from the essay and short report formats of Level 3 or 4 to the management report structure at Level 5, complete with executive summary, recommendations, and Evaluate-depth analysis, is a significant step. Tutoring provides the transition framework.

## What CMI Assignment Tutoring Includes

CMI assignment tutoring is available in three formats, depending on where you are in the assignment process and what kind of support you need.

### Assignment Planning: Structuring Before You Write

You send your unit brief and assignment question to your tutor. The tutor reviews the unit's Learning Outcomes, Assessment Criteria, and command verbs, then produces a written assignment outline: a section-by-section plan that shows you exactly what to write, in what order, and at what depth.

The deliverable is a written plan with a heading structure, direction notes for each section (including which management theories to apply and how), and guidance on word count distribution. You write to this plan and submit your own original work. This is the lowest-risk entry point for new tutoring clients. It costs less than full draft review and gives you a clear roadmap before you invest time writing.

For an understanding of the command verbs you'll be asked to apply, see our [CMI command verbs guide](/guides/cmi-command-verbs-explained/).

### Draft Review and Feedback: Improving What You've Written

You write your assignment draft and send it to your tutor. The tutor reviews it specifically against your unit's Assessment Criteria and produces written feedback identifying:

- Which Learning Outcomes are fully met, partially met, or not addressed
- Sections where the command verb depth is insufficient (e.g., where you've described when the task asks you to Evaluate)
- Referencing gaps: missing sources, incorrectly formatted citations, and insufficient academic evidence
- Structural improvements: sections out of sequence, arguments not connected, and a conclusion that doesn't reflect the analysis
- Language and academic register: where informal or operational language weakens the academic quality

You receive the feedback as a structured written document with specific, actionable corrections. You revise your draft independently and submit your own improved work.

### Resubmission Coaching: Getting Past Assessor Referral Feedback

You share your assessor's referral feedback, the written comments explaining which Learning Outcomes were not met and why. The tutor analyses the feedback, identifies exactly which sections of your assignment need to change, and provides a correction plan: what to rewrite, what theoretical angle to take, and what evidence to add.

This is targeted, surgical coaching. The goal is not to rewrite the entire assignment. It is to fix the specific elements the assessor has identified as insufficient, without disrupting the sections that already pass. CMI does allow resubmissions, and targeted resubmission coaching is typically far more effective than starting the unit from scratch.

## How Does Tutoring Help You Achieve a Merit or Distinction in CMI
The difference between a pass and a merit in CMI is almost always command verb depth. A pass response covers the Learning Outcomes at a surface level. It describes, identifies, and explains. A merit response evaluates, justifies, and analyses at the command verb at the cognitive depth the assessor expects.

A CMI tutor knows this distinction precisely. When reviewing a draft, the tutor identifies exactly where your response has described when the task asks you to evaluate, and shows you how to restructure that section to meet the merit standard. This is not generic writing improvement. It is CMI-specific academic coaching.

For distinction-level work, the tutor reviews whether your recommendations are SMART and strategically justified (not just added as an afterthought), whether your Evaluate responses include explicit criteria and evidence (not just a list of pros and cons), and whether your referencing draws on academic sources that carry weight with the assessor.

Many students who receive a pass on first submission and use tutoring for subsequent units see a consistent improvement in their grades because the tutor has equipped them with the framework for merit-level responses from that point forward.

## FAQ: CMI Assignment Tutoring

**What is the difference between CMI tutoring and CMI assignment writing**
CMI assignment tutoring supports your own writing. You write the assignment and the tutor guides your structure, command verb application, and Learning Outcome alignment. The writing service produces the assignment for you as a submission-ready document. Both options are available. Tutoring is the right choice for students who want to retain authorship and develop their academic skills. The writing service is the right choice for students who need a completed assignment delivered to a deadline.

**How long does a tutoring session take**
Tutoring is asynchronous. You send your brief or draft via WhatsApp and receive structured written feedback within 24–48 hours. There are no scheduled video calls required. If you prefer a live discussion, this can be arranged via WhatsApp call.

**Can you tutor me on a specific CMI command verb**
Yes. Command verb coaching is available as a standalone session. Send your unit brief and the specific command verb(s) your task uses, and your tutor will explain exactly how to apply them at the correct cognitive depth for your level and unit. This is particularly useful for students transitioning from Level 4 Analyse to Level 5 Evaluate for the first time.

**Can I get tutoring for a CMI resubmission**
Yes. Resubmission coaching is one of our most common tutoring requests. Share your assessor's referral feedback and we will identify exactly which Learning Outcomes were not met and what needs to be rewritten. This is typically faster and more effective than attempting a full rewrite from scratch.

**Is CMI tutoring available for all levels**
Yes. Tutoring is available for CMI Levels 3 to 7, including Award, Certificate, and Diploma qualifications, across all available units. The tutor is matched to your level and unit subject area.

**How much does CMI assignment tutoring cost**
Pricing depends on the session type. Planning sessions are less than full draft review, and resubmission coaching is priced based on the volume of feedback required. WhatsApp us with your unit, level, and session type for an instant transparent quote.
