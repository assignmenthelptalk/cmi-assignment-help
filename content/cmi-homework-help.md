## What Is CMI Homework?

CMI homework refers to any written task you are required to complete and submit for assessment as part of your CMI qualification. Your training provider or assessor sets the task — it may be called a homework exercise, a written activity, a unit assignment, or a coursework piece — but it is formally assessed against CMI's Learning Outcomes and Assessment Criteria.

CMI homework is not practice work. It counts toward your qualification result. It must meet the academic standard — the correct format, the appropriate command verb depth, Harvard referencing, and a response that satisfies the specific Learning Outcomes for your unit. A homework piece that describes rather than analyses, or that lacks academic referencing, will receive a referral regardless of how much management knowledge it demonstrates.

CMI homework is submitted to your training provider or assessor for marking. The training provider marks it against CMI's Assessment Criteria and submits results to CMI. The piece itself is not returned to CMI unless there is a quality audit — but the mark stands.

### Types of CMI Homework Tasks Our Service Covers

Our CMI homework help covers every format of written task that CMI qualifications include:

**Management reports** — Formal business documents with an executive summary, analysis sections, conclusion, and recommendations. Most common at Level 5 and above.

**Written essays** — Academic argument format using command verbs like Discuss and Analyse. Most common at Level 3 and Level 4.

**Reflective accounts** — First-person written reflections on your own professional practice or learning experience, structured using a reflective model like Gibbs' Reflective Cycle or Kolb's Experiential Learning model.

**Work-based activity responses** — Written responses that require you to apply management theory to a situation or challenge from your own workplace. The task is usually to describe the situation, explain what you did or would do, and justify your approach using management theory.

**Short written responses** — Concise answers to specific unit brief questions, often used in Level 3 and Level 4 units where the task is narrower in scope.

## Is CMI Homework the Same as a Formal CMI Assignment?

Yes — in all official CMI documentation, these pieces of work are called "assignments." Training providers often use different language — homework, written tasks, coursework, or activities — but they are referring to the same formally assessed written submissions.

The Assessment Criteria, Learning Outcomes, word count requirements, referencing standard, and format expectations are exactly the same regardless of what your training provider calls the task. A piece called "homework" that is assessed against CMI's Assessment Criteria is, functionally, a CMI assignment.

This also means that all the same rules apply. Harvard referencing is required. The correct academic format (essay or report) must be used. The command verbs in the task must be applied at the appropriate depth. Submitting descriptive writing for a task that asks you to Analyse will receive a referral — even if your training provider gave you the task as a homework exercise rather than a formal "assignment."
